EggSandwich
S1: Coat 6-oz. ramekin or custard cup with cooking spray.
S2: Place a piece of canadian bacon on the bottom of a 6-oz. ramekin or custard cup.
S3: Pour 1/4 cup egg substitute on top of the canadian bacon in ramekin or custard cup.
S4: Microwave the ramekin or custard cup uncovered on high for 30 seconds and stir.
S5: Continue to Microwave for 15-30 more seconds or until egg is almost set.
S6: Top with 1 tablespoon of salsa and sprinkle with 1 tablespoon of cheese.
S7: Microwave just until cheese melts, about 10 seconds.
S8: Cut the english muffin into two pieces with a knife.
S9: Line bottom of English muffin with lettuce.
S10: Take out egg and Canadian bacon from the cup, place it over lettuce and replace the top of english muffin.
BreakfastPotatoes
S1: Peel 2 medium potatoes.
S2: Slice 2 peeled medium potatoes.
S3: Peel one medium onion
S4: Slice one medium onion.
S5: Coat a 9-in. microwave-safe plate with cooking spray
S6: Arrange potato and onion slices on the plate
S7: Sprinkle potato and onion with seasonings (garlic salt, salt, pepper) on the plate.
S8: Cover the plate and microwave on high for 8 minutes and 30 seconds or until potatoes are tender
S9: Add 1/4 cup shredded cheddar cheese on the top and microwave for 30 seconds of cooking.
Dressed-UpMeatballs
S1: Cut 1/4 medium carrots into short, thin strips
S2: Peel 1 small onion.
S3: Slice 1/8 small onion
S4: Cut 1/8 small green pepper into short, thin strips
S5: Peel 1 garlic clove.
S6: Cut and Mince 1/8 garlic clove
S7: Place 1/4-pound meatballs in a 3-qt. Microwave-safe dish.
S8: Top with the carrots, onion, pepper and garlic.
S9: In a small bowl, mix 1.5 ounces sweet-and-sour sauce and 1/2 teaspoon soy sauce
S10: Pour the sauces over the meatballs.
S11: Microwave, covered, on high for 1.5 minutes
S12: Stir the contents in the microwave with a spoon.
S13: Microwave again, covered, on high for 1.5 minutes
S14: Stir the contents in the microwave with a spoon.
S15: Microwave for 1 more minute.

Ramen
S1: Remove the noodles from the package. (Break Noodles / Keep them as a block).
S2: Peel and Chop garlic on cutting board.
S3: Peel one small onion.
S4: Finely chop the peeled onion on cutting board.
S5: Take peas out of the bag and thaw the peas by putting it in a sieve and running it under some cold water.
S6: Put all the Vegetables in a microwave-safe bowl.
S7: Add the noodles to the bowl and cover them with water.
S8: Cover with a lid (or paper towel) to prevent splattering.
S9: Microwave the ramen for between three and five minutes.
S10: Measure basil and cilantro and add them to the bowl.
S11: Let the noodles sit for about three minutes after the microwave stops.

ApplePie
S1: Place the crust into a microwave-safe pie dish.
S2: Score (press) the bottom and sides of the crust with a fork.
S3: Microwave on high for 5 minutes and let it rest while you make the fillings.
S4: Take the apple, wash and then peel it.
S5: Slice the apple into pieces on top of a cutting board.
S6: Add the sliced apple to the microwave-safe bowl.
S7: Measure 3 tablespoons of flour and add it to the bowl.
S8: Measure 1/4 cup of white sugar and add it to the bowl.
S9: Measure 1/4 cup of brown sugar and add it to the bowl.
S10: Measure 1 teaspoon of cinnamon and add it to the bowl.
S11: Measure 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract and add it to the bowl.
S12: Extract the juice from 1/2 lemon and add it to the bowl.
S13: Mix all the ingredients well.
S14: Cover the bowl and then microwave on high for 2.5 minutes.
S15: Stir the contents in the bowl.
S16: Again, Microwave on high for 2.5 minutes.
S17: Add the filling (from the microwave) on top of the crust.
S18: Measure 1 cup of brown sugar and add it to the bowl.
S19: Measure 1/2 cup of flour and add it to the bowl.
S20: Measure 1/2 cup of quick oats and add it to the bowl.
S21: Cut cold butter (1/2 cup) and put it in the bowl.
S22: Add the crumble top (from the bowl) to the pie (with crust and filling).
S23: Microwave for 5 minutes and take the pie out.
Brownies
S1: Put 1/4 stick butter into a microwave-safe bowl.
S2: Add 0.75 oz (2.5 tsp) chocolate to the microwave-safe bowl.
S3: Heat the microwave-safe bowl on high for 1 to 2 minutes, stirring every 30 seconds to melt the chocolate (chocolate mixture).
S4: Break 1 egg in a bowl and throw away the shells.
S5: Take 1/4 cup of sugar and add it to the bowl.
S6: Whisk together the eggs and sugar (egg mixture).
S7: Measure 1/8 teaspoon of baking powder and add it to the another bowl.
S8: Measure 1/8 teaspoon of salt and add it to the bowl.
S9: Measure 1/8 cup of all-purpose flour and add it to the bowl.
S10: Add the egg and chocolate mixtures to the bowl.
S11: Measure 1/4 teaspoon of vanilla extract, add it to the bowl and stir the ingredients.
S12: Spray a microwave-safe glass pan with cooking spray.
S13: Pour the batter into the pan by spreading it out evenly.
S14: Measure 1/8 cup of chocolate chunks and scatter them on top.
S15: Cook in the microwave for 3 minutes.
S16: Remove from the microwave and rest for 2 minutes before cutting.
Coffee
S1: Measure 12 ounces of cold water and transfer to a kettle.
S2: Close the lid of the kettle and boil the water.
S3: While the water is boiling, assemble the filter cone.  Place the dripper on top of a coffee mug.
S4: Prepare the filter insert by folding the paper filter in half to create a semi-circle, and in half again to create a quarter-circle.
S5: Place the paper filter in the dripper and spread open to create a cone.
S6: Weigh the coffee beans
S7: Grind the coffee beans until the coffee grounds are the consistency of coarse sand, about 20 seconds.
S8: Transfer the grounds to the filter cone.
S9: Once the water has boiled, check the temperature of the water.  (The water should be between 195-205 degrees Fahrenheit or between 91-96 degrees Celsius.   If the water is too hot, let it cool briefly.)
S10: Pour a small amount of water in the filter to wet the grounds.  Wait about 30 seconds for coffee to bloom.  (You will see small bubbles or foam on the coffee grounds during this step.)
S11: Slowly pour the rest of the water over the grounds in a circular motion.  Do not overfill beyond the top of the paper filter.
S12: Let the coffee drain completely into the mug before removing the dripper.   Discard the paper filter and coffee grounds.
Pinwheels
S1: Place tortilla on cutting board.
S2: Use a butter knife to scoop nut butter from the jar. Spread nut butter onto the tortilla, leaving 1/2-inch uncovered at the edges.
S3: Clean the knife by wiping it with a paper towel.
S4: Use the knife to scoop jelly from the jar.  Spread jelly over the nut butter.
S5: Clean the knife by wiping with a paper towel.
S6: Roll the tortilla from one end to the other into a log shape, about 1.5 inches thick.  Roll it tight enough to prevent gaps, but not so tight that the filling leaks.
S7: Secure the rolled tortilla by inserting 5 toothpicks about 1 inch apart.
S8: Trim the ends of the tortilla roll with the butter knife, leaving Â½ inch margin between the last toothpick and the end of the roll.  Discard ends.
S9: Slide floss under the tortilla, perpendicular to the length of the roll.  Place the floss halfway between two toothpicks.
S10: Cross the floss's two ends over the tortilla roll's top. Holding one end of the floss in each hand, pull the floss ends in opposite directions to slice.
S11: Continue slicing with floss to create 1 more pinwheel.
S12: Continue slicing with floss to create 1 more pinwheel.
S13: Continue slicing with floss to create 1 more pinwheel.
S14: Place the pinwheels on a plate.
